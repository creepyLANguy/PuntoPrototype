The ecosystem

A complete smart court layer for clubs and players.

Padel Push combines intelligent hardware devices with a cloud-powered scoring platform. Players interact with physical devices on court while scores, matches, and device management sync instantly online.

Display scores anywhere

Use courtside TVs, tablets, club displays, mobile phones, web browsers, and kiosks as live scoreboards.

Built for real courts

Rechargeable batteries, USB-C power, smart Wi-Fi reconnects, offline setup recovery, and buzzer feedback keep the system practical in live club environments.

Ready to grow

Start with manual scoring, add hands-free triggers, then unlock club-wide device allocation and NFC powered interactions.

Devices

Purpose-built scoring hardware.

Each Padel Push device is designed for simplicity, portability, and reliable match-day use.

Portable scorer

Pulse

Direct player control with a tiny portable device designed for fast gameplay.

  • Single-button scoring
  • Hold actions for undo, switch teams, and reset
  • Audible score feedback
  • NFC registration support
  • Ideal for casual matches and club rentals
Hands-free scoring

Beacon

Automatic scoring interactions using distance sensing technology.

  • Distance-based score triggering
  • Physical team assignment switch
  • Minimal interaction required
  • Rechargeable battery
  • Ideal for high-traffic and competitive courts
Central control

Hub

The connection point for full smart court functionality and club-wide management.

  • NFC reader integration
  • Device-to-court registration
  • Court management tools
  • Smart device allocation
  • Ideal for multi-court venues
Upgrade path

Start simple. Scale naturally.

Padel Push grows with your club, from portable scorekeeping to a fully connected venue.

1

Pulse

Lend or rent portable Pulse devices to players for manual scoring with instant live scoreboards and minimal infrastructure.

2

Beacon

Add Beacon devices courtside for hands-free score triggering that reduces interruptions and simplifies gameplay interactions.

3

Hub

Integrate Hubs to manage devices, assign courts dynamically, and enable NFC-powered interactions across the venue.

FAQ

Quick answers.

Do players need phones to score?

No. Pulse and Beacon devices work independently on court.

Can scoreboards be viewed remotely?

Yes. Matches can be viewed from any internet-connected display.

Do devices work on battery power?

Yes. Devices include rechargeable batteries and USB-C support.

Can clubs start with only a few devices?

Absolutely. Padel Push is designed around a scalable upgrade path.
